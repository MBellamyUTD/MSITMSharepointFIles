The Hiring Funnel

Understanding what happens before the interview — and where most candidates are eliminated.

Where Are Candidates Actually Eliminated?

There is a common misconception among graduate students: if you are strong technically, you will get the interview. In practice, hiring decisions at the early stage have almost nothing to do with technical depth.

Applications come in. ATS screens first. Then a recruiter reviews. Then HR evaluates. Then interviews. Then offers.

Most candidates are eliminated before technical depth is evaluated.

Early-stage hiring decisions are about alignment, clarity, communication, and perceived fit — not your GitHub portfolio.

Stage What Is Evaluated What You Control
ATS Keywords, formatting, alignment with job description Resume structure, terminology, and tailoring
Recruiter Screen Communication clarity, role alignment, confidence, business awareness Storytelling, preparation, and structured answers
HR Review Professionalism, culture fit, responsiveness Tone, maturity, and follow-through
Hiring Manager Technical depth and structured problem-solving Impact framing and business outcome clarity
🔑

You are not just competing against a job description. You are competing against other candidates — many of whom are technically similar to you. What differentiates finalists is preparation, clarity, and how well they communicate value.

What This Means for Your Strategy

At each stage of the hiring funnel, something different is being evaluated — and you have different levers to pull.

  • ATS Does not evaluate potential. It matches patterns. If your resume does not use the right keywords in the right format, it may never reach a human.
  • Recruiters Are asking one core question: Would I feel confident presenting this candidate to the hiring manager?
  • HR Is evaluating professionalism and fit — not code. Tone, responsiveness, and how you carry yourself matter.
  • Hiring Managers Evaluate structured thinking and the ability to connect technical work to business outcomes.

The modules that follow give you specific strategies for each stage.
